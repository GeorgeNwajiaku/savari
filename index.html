📐 Measurement Guide

Select your outfit type below for tailored measurement guidance. All measurements are in inches. Use a soft tape measure and have someone assist you for accuracy.

🧍

Stand upright. Relax arms at your sides. Wear fitted clothing or measure over light fabric only.

Chest

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape level and parallel to the floor.

Waist

Measure around your natural waistline, about 1 inch above the belly button. Breathe naturally — don't suck in.

Shoulder Width

Measure across the back from the tip of one shoulder to the other, following the shoulder line.

Sleeve Length

Measure from the shoulder seam down the outside of the arm to the wrist bone, with arm slightly bent.

Body Length

Measure from the highest point of the shoulder down to your desired outfit hem length.

Inseam

For trousers — measure from the crotch seam to the ankle bone along the inside of the leg.

🧍‍♀️

Stand naturally. Wear fitted undergarments only. Tie a ribbon at your natural waist as a reference point.

Bust

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape level. Don't pull too tight.

Waist

Measure around the narrowest part of your torso — usually 1–2 inches above the navel.

Hip

Measure around the fullest part of your hips and bottom, typically 7–9 inches below your natural waist.

Shoulder Width

Measure across the back from shoulder tip to shoulder tip along the shoulder seam.

Dress / Skirt Length

Measure from your natural waistline down to your desired hem length (knee, midi, or floor).

Sleeve Length

From the shoulder point to your wrist, with arm slightly bent. Required for long-sleeve styles.

👘

Kaftans and boubous are flowing garments. Focus on chest, shoulder, and total length for the best fit.

Chest (for fit opening)

Measure around the fullest part of your chest. The kaftan will be cut with ease for comfort.

Shoulder Width

Critical for kaftan fit — measure shoulder to shoulder across the back at the widest point.

Kaftan Length

Measure from the shoulder top down to your desired length — ankle-length is traditional; knee-length is modern.

Sleeve Width & Length

For wide-sleeve boubous, state preferred sleeve width at the opening. Sleeve length from shoulder to wrist.

Neck Opening

Specify your neck/collar size for embroidery and cut placement. Standard kaftan necks are round or V-shaped.

🎭

Agbada and Senator sets require precise measurements across multiple pieces — the outer robe, inner shirt, and trousers.

Chest

Measure around the fullest part of the chest for the inner shirt (buba). Add 4–6 inches for the outer agbada robe.

Waist & Hip

Required for the trousers (sokoto). Waist at the natural line; hip at the fullest point of the seat.

Agbada Robe Length

Full-length agbada typically falls to the ankle. Measure from shoulder to desired hem length.

Buba (inner shirt) Length

Usually falls to mid-thigh. Measure from shoulder down to desired length — state if you prefer longer or shorter.

Trouser Inseam

Measure along the inside of the leg from crotch to ankle. Senator trousers are usually slim-fitted.

Cap Size

Measure the circumference of your head just above the ears. Most caps use this for fila / cap sizing.

👨‍👩‍👧

For family and couple matching sets, create a separate profile for each person. Use the standard guides above for each member's outfit type.

Create Profiles for Each Person

Use the "+ Add Profile" button above to create profiles for your spouse, children, and other family members individually.

Children's Measurements

For children: measure chest, waist, hip, shoulder, total height, and arm length. Note child's age as a reference.

Matching Fabric & Colour

State the same fabric and colour code for all profiles in your order so the tailor sources from the same batch.

Group Order Note

In the Special Instructions field, reference "Family Set — 4 pieces" so the tailor coordinates production together.

⚠️ Important: Measurements should reflect your actual body size. Add 1–2 inches for comfort unless requesting a fitted style. If your measurements seem unusual, our tailor will reach out to confirm before production begins. Savari is not responsible for errors in self-reported measurements — please measure carefully.
